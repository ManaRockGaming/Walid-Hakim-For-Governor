Investing In Education and South Carolina's Future

Hakim believes that a strong state begins with strong education. Instead of cutting funding, South Carolina must invest deeply in its schools and students.

He proposes the creation of “South Carolina College Investment Grants” for students pursuing critical careers; such as nursing, medicine, engineering, education, social work, and therapy. Rather than burdening graduates with debilitating student loans, this plan allows them to repay the state through service. Every graduate who accepts a grant would commit to living and working in South Carolina for 10–20 years; as taxpayers, homeowners, consumers, and parents. Their contributions to the state’s economy and communities would serve as repayment for their education. This initiative would ensure that the most educated and capable workforce in the world lives right here in South Carolina, making its citizens highly sought after in every field imaginable.

Reshaping the School System

Nutritious Meals for Every Student: Every school in South Carolina would provide healthy breakfast and lunch to all students, ensuring no child goes hungry during the school day.

Statewide Childcare Integration: Childcare would be incorporated into the state school system, giving working parents reliable and affordable early education options.

Year-Round Schooling: Schools would transition to a year-round schedule. This program would not add any days to the school calendar. It would eliminate the need for student review at the beginning of every school year, allowing the students to transition to the next year without a long summer break. This would result in students graduating by the completion of the 10th grade.

Career and College Readiness:Grades 11 and 12 would be transformed into trade school or associate degree programs, giving students a head start on their careers or higher education.

Protecting Schools, Ending the School-to-Prison Pipeline

As Governor, he would end the reliance on school resource officers and redirect that funding toward safety improvements.

Every classroom in South Carolina would be equipped with spring safety locking, bullet-resistant doors. These upgrades would protect students and teachers without criminalizing young people or perpetuating the school-to-prison pipeline. The discipline of students should be managed by the school administration and staff, unless law enforcement is requested. We do not need law enforcement inserting themselves when these situation can be resolved by school staff. Through smarter investment in infrastructure, education, and opportunity, Hakim envisions a South Carolina where schools are safe, nurturing, and built for every child’s success.

Modern Transit and Smart Infrastructure

To quote the Mayor of Bogota, Columbia: “A developed country is not a place where the poor have cars. It’s where the rich use public transport.”

Hakim believes this vision captures the future South Carolina deserves; a state where public transportation is fast, clean, and free for all. As Governor of South Carolina, Hakim would contract the development of a high-speed rail systems connecting the state’s major cities, creating a seamless network from Charleston, to Columbia, Atlanta, and many more.

Complementing this effort, the state would introduce a fleet of solar powered, no cost public buses to transport citizens to and from work. This program would be designed to benefit both employers and employees; reducing parking needs, cutting road congestion, and saving South Carolina hundreds of millions of dollars every year in infrastructure and maintenance costs. Through these innovations, Hakim envisions a South Carolina that leads the South in sustainable growth, smart design, and efficient mobility. We need a state built for the people, not for traffic.

Limiting Property Hoarding

To keep housing affordable and communities strong, Hakim would limit property ownership to ten residential properties per corporation or individual, excluding apartment complexes.This policy ensures that homes remain in the hands of residents, not investors, preventing mass buyouts that drive up housing costs and displace families. By curbing speculative ownership, South Carolina can stabilize neighborhoods, lower rents, and make homeownership attainable again for working people across the state.

Affordable Healthcare for Every South Carolinian

With health insurance rates expected to double or triple next year, Hakim proposes creating the Carolina Insurance Company: a state-owned insurer, offering full coverage to every resident. For $100 a month per individual or $200 per family, South Carolinians would receive medical, dental, and vision coverage with no deductibles, no co-pays, and assistance for seniors, disabled, veterans, and low-income families.

All licensed medical professionals would be required to accept the plan as in-network, and new procedures would never be denied. Every child born in South Carolina would be automatically enrolled for life. By keeping healthcare affordable and accessible, the Carolina Insurance Company would save families money, support businesses, and build a healthier South Carolina.

Affordable Auto Insurance for Every South Carolinian

With insurance rates rising and not covering the entire expense of repairs, many South Carolinans are forced to make touch decisions. With the creation of the Carolina Insurance Company, you can purchase auto insurance that will cover all incidents and injuries.

Homeowners & Renters Insurance for Every South Carolinian

We will offer policies to every South Carolinian. This policy will protect South Carolinians from all disasters or loss of property, regardless of the source.

Support For Police That Support Our Citizens

As Governor of South Carolina, Hakim would reform the police to benefit the people of South Carolina, in addition to the all the men and women who wear the uniform. We have failed the law enforcement community by not providing them with the tools they need to do their job on a daily basis. As Governor, Hakim would increase the training of all police officers to 18 months, giving them an opporunity to gain a degree in law enforcement, select on the job training, and to provide the support staff necessary. As Governor, he would hire one social worker for every ten sworn police officers, who would handle mental health emergenices and provide support to victims of violent crimes; this would allow police to concentrate on law enforcement and allow them to return to duty while the social worker support staff takes over victim services.

Smart and Safe Firearm Policy

As governor of South Carolina, Hakim would offer grants for qualified citizens to purchase rifles for hunting or home protection. Each person would be required to take a gun safety course, submit to a background check, and evaluation to qualify for this voluntary grant. Military veterans and law enforcement would be excempt from the grant process, but can still apply. As a candidate, Hakim wants to make clear he has no intention of confiscating guns or implementing any additional restrictions that are in place.

Hakim's 100 Year Plan for South Carolina

While we cannot predict the future, we can prepare for it. And while we prepare, we must build safeguards creating a South Carolina that’s worth saving, one that is safe for our grandchildren and does not sacrifice the tomorrows of the future for the indulgences of today.

Hakim’s vision is to develop a 100 Year Plan for South Carolina: a roadmap for a sustainable, prosperous, and resilient state. This plan will invest in sustainable infrastructure that becomes the lifeline of commerce, trade, preserving South Carolina’s remaining forests as sanctuaries for native wildlife. We will evolve our state education so every student can graduate debt-free making South Carolina a national model for higher learning.

With planning, we have hope. With hope, we have a chance. With this last chance, we, the Green Party, will accomplish our goal of saving the future for generations.
