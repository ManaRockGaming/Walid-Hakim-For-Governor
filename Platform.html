POLICY PLATFORM

Investing In Education and South Carolina's Future

Hakim believes that a strong state begins with strong education. Instead of cutting funding, South Carolina must invest deeply in its schools and students.

He proposes the creation of “South Carolina College Investment Grants” for students pursuing critical careers; such as nursing, medicine, engineering, education, social work, and therapy. Rather than burdening graduates with debilitating student loans, this plan allows them to repay the state through service. Every graduate who accepts a grant would commit to living and working in South Carolina for 10–20 years; as taxpayers, homeowners, consumers, and parents. Their contributions to the state’s economy and communities would serve as repayment for their education. This initiative would ensure that the most educated and capable workforce in the world lives right here in South Carolina, making its citizens highly sought after in every field imaginable.

Reshaping the School System

Nutritious Meals for Every Student: Every school in South Carolina would provide healthy breakfast and lunch to all students, ensuring no child goes hungry during the school day.

Statewide Childcare Integration: Childcare would be incorporated into the state school system, giving working parents reliable and affordable early education options.

Year-Round Schooling: Schools would transition to a year-round schedule. This program would not add any days to the school calendar. It would eliminate the need for student review at the beginning of every school year, allowing the students to transition to the next year without a long summer break. This would result in students graduating by the completion of the 10th grade.

Career and College Readiness: Grades 11 and 12 would be transformed into trade school or associate degree programs, giving students a head start on their careers or higher education.

Protecting Schools, Ending the School-to-Prison Pipeline

As Governor, he would reduce the reliance on school resource officers and redirect that funding toward safety improvements.

Every classroom in South Carolina would be equipped with spring safety locking, bullet-resistant doors. These upgrades would protect students and teachers without criminalizing young people or perpetuating the school-to-prison pipeline. The discipline of students should be managed by the school administration and staff, unless law enforcement is requested. We do not need law enforcement inserting themselves when these situation can be resolved by school staff. Through smarter investment in infrastructure, education, and opportunity, Hakim envisions a South Carolina where schools are safe, nurturing, and built for every child’s success.

Protecting Children

Hakim is the only candidate for governor that is in favor of raising the age of consent from 16 to 18 for all South Carolinians. If politicians were truly sincere about about protecting our children, it should be all of them, not just Hakim. Hakim's challenge to all of his fellow candidates, to plagiarize or steal this platform policy to benefit our state.

Affordable Healthcare for Every South Carolinian

With health insurance rates expected to double or triple next year, Hakim proposes creating the Carolina Insurance Company: a state-owned insurer, offering full coverage to every resident. For $100 a month per individual or $200 per family, South Carolinians would receive medical, dental, and vision coverage with no deductibles, no co-pays, and assistance for seniors, disabled, veterans, and low-income families.

All licensed medical professionals would be required to accept the plan as in-network, and new procedures would never be denied. Every child born in South Carolina would be automatically enrolled for life. By keeping healthcare affordable and accessible, the Carolina Insurance Company would save families money, support businesses, and build a healthier South Carolina.

Affordable Auto Insurance for Every South Carolinian

With insurance rates rising and not covering the entire expense of repairs, many South Carolinans are forced to make touch decisions. With the creation of the Carolina Insurance Company, you can purchase auto insurance that will cover all incidents and injuries.

Homeowners & Renters Insurance for Every South Carolinian

We will offer policies to every South Carolinian. This policy will protect South Carolinians from all disasters or loss of property, regardless of the source.

Limiting Property Hoarding

To keep housing affordable and communities strong, Hakim would limit property ownership to twelve residential properties per corporation or individual, excluding apartment complexes.This policy ensures that homes remain in the hands of residents, not investors, preventing mass buyouts that drive up housing costs and displace families. By curbing speculative ownership, South Carolina can stabilize neighborhoods, lower rents, and make homeownership attainable again for working people across the state.

Modern Transit and Smart Infrastructure

To quote the Mayor of Bogota, Columbia: “A developed country is not a place where the poor have cars. It’s where the rich use public transport.”

Hakim believes this vision captures the future South Carolina deserves; a state where public transportation is fast, clean, and free for all. As Governor of South Carolina, Hakim would contract the development of a high-speed rail systems connecting the state’s major cities, creating a seamless network from Charleston, to Columbia, Atlanta, and many more.

Complementing this effort, the state would introduce a fleet of solar powered, no cost public buses to transport citizens to and from work. This program would be designed to benefit both employers and employees; reducing parking needs, cutting road congestion, and saving South Carolina hundreds of millions of dollars every year in infrastructure and maintenance costs. Through these innovations, Hakim envisions a South Carolina that leads the South in sustainable growth, smart design, and efficient mobility. We need a state built for the people, not for traffic.

Protecting South Carolina's Green Spaces

As Governor of South Carolina, Hakim would audit all treaties, agreements, and promises made by the State of South Carolina and the USA to bring our state into full compliance with all Native American tribes in our state. Futhermore, Hakim would invite the leadership of the tribes to help us manage and preserve all publicly owned green spaces, preserving them for future generations. Hakim would also strive to be more inclusive in state government while acknowledging the sovereignty of all tribes in South Carolina.

Support For Police That Support Our Citizens

As governor of South Carolina, Hakim would take many steps to restructure law enforcement. We have created an unnecessary￼ burden on law enforcement by envisioning the “do it all cop.” Law enforcement, traffic enforcement, first responder to car crashes, first responder to attempted suicide, first responder to mental health emergencies, first responder to animal attacks, and the list goes on. Police were not trained to respond to every situation, yet they are expected to. This creates a dangerous situation for both law enforcement and the general public when we send people to do jobs they were not trained to do. By mandating the hiring, (academy) training, and employment of SLED social workers deployed with local law enforcement, we can allow police to get back to doing what they were hired to do: enforce laws. Social workers would respond to nonviolent mental health situations and work as a victims' advocate for domestic violence, child abuse/endangerment, mental health emergencies and rape. The social worker would complete paperwork related to these events and act as the victims advocate, which would allow police to get back to what they were trained to do more quickly: enforce the laws they were sworn to uphold.

Smart and Safe Firearm Policy

As governor of South Carolina, Hakim would offer grants for qualified citizens to purchase rifles for hunting or home protection. Each person would be required to take a gun safety course, submit to a background check, and evaluation to qualify for this voluntary grant. Military veterans and law enforcement would be excempt from the grant process, but can still apply. As a candidate, Hakim wants to make clear he has no intention of confiscating guns or implementing any additional restrictions that are in place.

Walid's 10 Point Plan - "A South Carolina, for South Carolinians"

By: Walid N. Hakim

Green Party Candidate for South Carolina Governor


I. HOUSING & ECONOMIC REFORM

The Carolina Bank (Nonprofit, Public-Owned)

• Create a state-owned nonprofit bank that provides affordable mortgages for homes, condos, townhouses, and sustainable villages.

• No evictions, no repossessions, no NSF fees. The mission: keep families permanently housed.

• End residential property hoarding: no individual or entity may own more than 12 single-family residential properties.

• Excess properties must be sold to SC residents or purchased by the state (at market value) via eminent domain.

• Protect homeownership from Wall Street firms and foreign investors.

Sustainable Villages

• Build hundreds of storm-resistant, high-quality communities with:

• Affordable housing

• Utilities included

• Grocery stores, gardens, public transit, community centers

• A model for climate-resilient, people-first living.

Carolina First Investment Policy

• End investment of SC taxpayer money in foreign nations or war-related bonds (e.g., ending purchases of Israel bonds).

• Redirect all state investment funds back into South Carolina infrastructure, housing, education, and savings.


II. HEALTHCARE INSURANCE FOR ALL

Carolina Insurance Company (State-Owned, Nonprofit)

• $100/month for individuals; $200/month for families.

• No co-pays, no deductibles, no denials.

• Dental & vision included.

• Every medical provider must accept Carolina Insurance to maintain an SC medical license.

• Children enrolled automatically for life.

Medicaid Expansion

• Accept all available federal funds (over $15.8 billion previously lost).

• Principle should never outweigh the lives and health of citizens.

Cost Transparency & Consumer Protection

• Create a statewide database of average prices for medical procedures, auto repairs, and additional services.

• Require businesses to post tax-inclusive, all-fees-included pricing for all goods and services.


III. CRIMINAL JUSTICE, POLICING, & REFORM

Modernizing Law Enforcement

• Deploy SLED-certified social workers alongside local police for:

• Domestic violence

• Child abuse

• Mental health crises

• Nonviolent emergencies

• Let police focus on enforcement; social workers handle victim advocacy and paperwork.

End the Prison Merry-Go-Round – “Keep Them Home Bill”

• Provide every inmate with:

• Trade certifications before release

• Temporary housing

• Job placement prior to release

• Wage-matching funds to cover basic needs

• Reduce the $1+ billion spent annually on prisons by cutting recidivism.

Drug Reform

• Release and expunge all records for personal-use drug possession.

• Legalize cannabis and decriminalize small quantities of select drugs.

• Focus on violent crime, not victimless offenses.


IV. EDUCATION TRANSFORMATION

Year-Round Schools & Earlier Graduation

• Keep total school days the same but adopt a year-round calendar.

• Graduation at 10th grade.

• Grades 11–12 become trade school or associate degree programs.

Childcare Integrated Into Every Public School

• State-funded childcare at all schools to support working families.

Breakfast & Lunch for All Students

College Investment Grants Instead of Loans

• Tuition-free college for critical professions: nurses, doctors, engineers, teachers, social workers, therapists, and more.

• Graduates repay through living, working, and paying taxes in South Carolina for 10–20 years.


V. INFRASTRUCTURE & LONG-TERM PLANNING

High-Quality Infrastructure

• End “lowest-bidder, lowest-quality” contracting.

• Require high-grade, long-lasting materials for roads, bridges, and public works.

• Save billions by reducing constant repairs and failures.

100-Year Plan for South Carolina

• Ensure environmental, economic, and educational sustainability for the next century.

• Protect remaining forests and green spaces, managed jointly with SC tribal nations.

Mass Transit + High-Speed Rail

• Build high-speed rail connecting major SC cities.

• Solar-powered, free-to-ride buses to reduce congestion and save hundreds of millions.


VI. SOCIAL VALUES, CIVIL RIGHTS & LIBERTIES

Reproductive Freedom

• Full support for a woman’s right to choose, including access to abortion.

• Being “pro-life” means supporting families after birth with childcare, healthcare, housing, and basic needs.

Separation of Church & State

• Protect religious freedom by keeping religion out of politics.

• Reject laws based on religious doctrine.

Protect Free Speech

• No book bans.

• No flag bans.

• Free speech must include speech you disagree with.

LGBTQ+, Minority, and Immigrant Rights

• Strong civil-rights enforcement for all communities.

• Reject cooperation with abuses by ICE; uphold the values of “The New Colossus.”

Native American Sovereignty

• Review all treaties and agreements for compliance.

• Partner with tribes in stewardship of public green spaces.

Raise the Age of Consent

VIII. GOVERNMENT ETHICS & ACCOUNTABILITY

Integrity in Leadership

Tax-Exempt Religious Organizations

Hemp & Bamboo Industrial Expansion

School Castles for Student Safety
